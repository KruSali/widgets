News: New Banknotes

Cost-Saving & Eco-Friendly, Launching Nov 21

Basic Version Summary

Thailand will have new 50 and 100 baht banknotes starting November 21. They are made of a special plastic called polymer. These new notes are stronger, stay cleaner, and last longer, which is good for the environment because fewer need to be printed.

These new notes are very hard to copy because they have special features. The most special part is a 'clear window' that you can look through. Inside this window, you can feel a raised number for the value (50 or 100). Also, if you tilt the note, some parts will change color. There are also special marks for people who cannot see well, so they can feel the value of the note. You can still use the old paper banknotes as usual.

Advanced Version Summary

The Bank of Thailand has announced new 50 and 100 baht polymer banknotes, set to enter circulation on November 21. Made from a durable polymer substrate, they are more resistant to dirt and moisture than their paper counterparts, leading to positive environmental impacts and cost savings.

To prevent counterfeiting, the notes incorporate advanced security technologies. A key feature is a large, clear polymer window that is difficult to replicate. Within this window, an embossed denomination serves as a visual and tactile verification point. Furthermore, they utilize optically variable ink, a hallmark of secure currency. For inclusivity, enhanced tactile features assist the visually impaired. Existing paper notes remain legal tender.
