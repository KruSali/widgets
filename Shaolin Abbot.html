Basic Version: Shaolin Abbot Under Investigation

On July 27, the Shaolin Temple announced that Shi Yongxin, its abbot, is being investigated. The investigation is about money and his personal life.

He is accused of taking temple money for himself from different projects. He is also said to have broken Buddhist rules by having secret relationships with several women and having children.

Shi Yongxin was born in 1965 and became a monk at Shaolin Temple in 1981. He became the abbot in 1999.

He was the first Chinese monk to get an MBA. He started doing business in 1998, covering tourism, culture, herbal medicine, and online sales.

In 2004, he reopened the "Shaolin Pharmacy." They sold old herbal products like pain relief patches and special teas, which brought in a lot of money for the temple.

In 2008, he also opened an online shop on Taobao. He sold religious items and "Zen lifestyle" products, making a lot of sales.

He also used platform Douyin (China's TikTok) in 2021 to teach martial arts and sell products live.

Shi Yongxin used to lead Shaolin martial arts monks to perform around the world over 200 times a year. Each show could earn up to $50,000 USD.

He also allowed others to use the "Shaolin" name for online games, music, and even "blessed" phone numbers to raise money.

The Shaolin Temple has almost 800 registered trademarks in many areas, like food, clothes, and hotels. Some sources say the temple earns about 300 million yuan a year from these businesses.

Shi Yongxin once controlled 18 companies. But now, he has stepped down from most of them.

In 2022, one of his related companies bought expensive land, which led to a lot of criticism. So, he removed his name from the company that year.

He was also accused of similar personal issues in 2015, but Chinese officials said it was not true then. However, in 2025, he was officially charged with criminal offenses for the first time.

Advanced Version: The Allegations Against Shaolin Abbot Shi Yongxin

On July 27, the Shaolin Temple issued a statement confirming that Shi Yongxin, its revered abbot, is currently under investigation by multiple authorities for serious criminal allegations.

The accusations include embezzlement of temple assets from various projects and severe violations of monastic discipline, specifically involving clandestine relationships with multiple women and fathering children.

Shi Yongxin, originally named Liu Yingcheng, was born in 1965. He was ordained at the Shaolin Temple in 1981 and ascended to the position of abbot in 1999.

He is recognized as a pioneering figure in China's commercialized Buddhism, being the first Chinese monk to obtain an MBA. His commercial ventures began in 1998, encompassing tourism, culture, herbal medicine, publishing, and e-commerce.

In 2004, he revitalized the "Shaolin Pharmacy," adapting ancient formulas into products like medicated plasters and Ganoderma lucidum tea, generating over 80 million yuan in revenue by 2019.

By 2008, he launched an online store on Taobao, selling religious artifacts and "Zen lifestyle" products, achieving sales exceeding 23 million yuan by 2020.

He further expanded into new media, utilizing Douyin (China's TikTok) in 2021 to share martial arts knowledge and conduct live-streamed sales personally.

Shi Yongxin previously led Shaolin warrior monk troupes on over 200 international tours annually, with individual performances recently fetching up to $50,000 USD.

He also licensed the "Shaolin" brand for online games, music, and even "blessed" mobile phone numbers for auction, generating additional revenue.

Records indicate the Shaolin Temple has registered 795 trademarks across diverse sectors including food, apparel, education, and hospitality.

Sources suggest the temple's annual commercial revenue approaches 300 million yuan.

Shi Yongxin once wielded control over 18 companies, playing a significant role in managing cultural assets, art exchange services, tourism planning, and investments in various enterprises like tea and cultural arts.

In 2022, a subsidiary's successful bid for land in Zhengzhou valued at 452 million yuan sparked widespread criticism, leading to his divestment from the company's shareholding in April of the same year.

Post-2022, Shi Yongxin has progressively divested from various companies. Currently, he remains a legal representative for only five monastic or Buddhist associations, with half of his previously associated companies having been revoked or dissolved.

While Shi Yongxin faced similar allegations regarding a wife and child in 2015, Chinese authorities at the time dismissed them as false. However, 2025 marks the first official criminal indictment against him.
