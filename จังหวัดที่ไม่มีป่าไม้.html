สรุปข่าวเวอร์ชั่นขั้นสูง

A surprising revelation from a 2024 report indicates that two Thai provinces, Nonthaburi and Pathum Thani, are completely devoid of forest land. The report states that Thailand's overall forest cover has slightly declined to 31.46%.

This figure falls short of the national target of 40% and highlights ongoing issues like encroachment. This downward trend threatens biodiversity. On a positive note, the data shows signs of reforestation in some areas, such as Ang Thong province.
