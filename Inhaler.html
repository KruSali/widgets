Part 1: Ancient Origins

The idea for yadom is very old. It comes from aromatherapy. In ancient Egypt, people would smell special plants to help them breathe.

Part 2: Thai Royal Traditions

In old Thailand, people in the palace made sweet smells called "Bunga Rampai". During King Rama V's time, yadom became very popular. A famous one was "Yadom Som Mue," made with a special fruit.

In Thailand, the tradition of crafting aromatic blends existed in the royal court since the late Ayutthaya period. The reign of King Rama V is considered a "golden age" for Thai aromatherapy, leading to many famous remedies, invented by a royal consort.
