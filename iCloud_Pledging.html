iCloud Pledging: A New Age Scam

ครูสาลี่ -- อังกฤษสำหรับคนเรียนไม่รู้เรื่อง

เกิดอะไรขึ้น? (What's Happening?)

Have you heard about iCloud pledging? It's a new and dangerous type of scam that is happening often. Recently in Thailand, specifically in June, the **Central Investigation Bureau (CIB)** raided four places in Bangkok and Samut Prakan. These places were companies involved in iCloud pledging, offering loans with very high interest rates, over 365% per year! Bad people try to trick others, especially those who own iPhones, to lose access to their device and data.

วิธีการของมิจฉาชีพ (How the Scammers Work)

Here's how it often works: A person needs money quickly and wants to pawn their phone. The scammer (the bad person) pretends to be a helpful pawn shop owner or a loan provider. They advertise on social media. They tell the victim to log in to iCloud using the scammer's own phone or computer. They would collect the victim's important personal data, including their Apple ID and password. Sometimes, they ask the victim to enable "Find My iPhone" on their device while the scammer holds it.

Once the victim logs in or activates the feature, the scammer uses the "Find My iPhone" service to remotely lock the device. This means the victim cannot use their phone anymore. If the victim misses a payment for 7 days, their phone gets locked. A message often appears on the locked screen, asking for more money to unlock it.

ผลกระทบและสิ่งของกลางที่ยึดได้ (The Impact & Seized Items)

Later, when the victim tries to regain possession of their phone or pay back the loan, the scammers refuse to unlock the iCloud. They demand more money, an extra ransom, to release the phone. This leaves the phone owner in a difficult situation, unable to access their data or use their device.

The police seized items like computers, mobile phones, and pawn documents. They found that there were over 5,000 customers or victims. The police are now pressing charges for charging illegal interest rates and operating a loan business without a license.

วิธีป้องกัน (How to Protect Yourself)

The **Economic Crime Suppression Division (ECD)** of the police warns everyone to only borrow money from legally certified financial places. It's very important to be extremely cautious. Never share your Apple ID and password with anyone. Your iCloud account is like a key to all your personal information and devices. If someone asks you to log into your iCloud on their device, or asks for your password, it's a big red flag. You risk being spied on, having data hacked, or even blackmailed without knowing it. Always keep your digital life safe!

ประเด็นเร่งด่วน (A Pressing Issue)

The proliferation of iCloud pledging scams has emerged as a significant cybercrime trend, primarily preying on vulnerable individuals who are in urgent need of financial liquidity. A recent and prominent case in Thailand, reported in June, involved a raid by the **Central Investigation Bureau (CIB)'s Economic Crime Suppression Division (ECD)**. They launched "Operation iLockdown," targeting four locations in Bangkok and Samut Prakan linked to iCloud pledging operations. These companies were found to be extending loans with exorbitant interest rates, exceeding 365% per annum. This elaborate form of digital extortion disproportionately affects iPhone users due to the integrated nature of Apple's ecosystem.

กลวิธีของมิจฉาชีพ (Scammers' Modus Operandi)

The typical modus operandi involves fraudsters, often posing as legitimate pawn brokers or lending agents, convincing victims to use their iCloud account as collateral for the loan. They widely advertised their services through social media platforms. Crucially, they would compel victims to furnish their Apple ID and password, or to authenticate their iCloud accounts on a third-party device (controlled by the scammer), or to enable the "Find My iPhone" feature while the victim's device is in the scammer's temporary possession. Upon gaining access, the criminals swiftly remotely lock the device using the Find My service. If a default on payment occurred for 7 days, the device would be locked. This remote lock effectively transforms the device into a digital hostage, displaying a custom message that demands payment for its release. This critical step forms the foundation of their subsequent extortion attempts.

ผลลัพธ์และความยากลำบากทางกฎหมาย (Consequences and Legal Predicament)

When victims return to redeem their device as per the initial agreement, the scammers then withhold the iCloud unlock, demanding additional payments – often a hefty ransom – beyond the agreed-upon pawn amount. This leaves the victims in a severe predicament, as their personal device, along with all its stored data, becomes inaccessible, creating significant financial and personal distress.

During the recent operation, authorities confiscated key evidence including computers, mobile phones, and pawn documents. Investigations revealed a staggering number of over 5,000 clients affected. The perpetrators are being prosecuted for serious offenses, including charging interest rates exceeding legal limits and operating a lending business without a proper license.

มาตรการป้องกัน (Preventive Measures)

The **Economic Crime Suppression Division (ECD)** emphatically advises the public to procure loans exclusively from duly certified financial institutions. This scam underscores the paramount importance of heightened cybersecurity awareness. Users must exercise extreme vigilance and diligently safeguard their login credentials for all cloud services, especially those linked to sensitive personal data like Apple ID. Any unsolicited request to authenticate your personal accounts on an unfamiliar device, or to share your passwords, should be immediately recognized as a serious security risk and vehemently declined. Failure to do so risks unauthorized surveillance, data breaches, or even unwitting blackmail. Prioritizing the security of your Apple ID and understanding the implications of granting access to your cloud data are fundamental steps in mitigating these risks and proactively preventing future instances of such digital fraud.
