ไขประเด็นร้อนภาษีโลก!

เมื่อนโยบายการค้าสหรัฐฯ สะเทือนถึงเศรษฐกิจไทย... เราได้หรือเสีย?

ครูสาลี่ -- อังกฤษฉบับคนเรียนไม่รู้เรื่อง

U.S. Announces New Tariffs

The U.S. President has announced new tariffs for many countries. Tariffs are taxes on imported goods. Some countries face very high tariffs, like Canada at 50%. But for Thailand, it is good news. Thailand's tariff rate was reduced to 19%. This is lower than the 36% that was threatened before. The Thai government is very happy about this.

A "Win-Win" for Thailand

A government spokesperson said this is a "win-win" situation. This new rate helps Thailand sell its products to the U.S. more easily and compete with other countries.

  • Vietnam: 20%
  • Indonesia: 19%
  • Philippines: 19%
  • Malaysia: 19%
  • Laos: 40%

U.S. Imposes Sweeping Tariffs

The U.S. administration has imposed significant new tariffs on numerous trading partners ahead of a crucial negotiation deadline. An executive order detailed the new rates, with some countries facing steep increases, such as Canada at 50%. The President stated some nations had failed to adequately address trade imbalances. In a separate move, tariffs on Canada were raised over a dispute concerning fentanyl control, which contrasted sharply with a temporary reprieve granted to Mexico.

A Strategic Victory for Thailand

In a notable development, Thailand's potential tariff rate was reduced to 19%, down from a threatened 36%. The Thai government hailed this as a "win-win" outcome. A spokesperson stated that the deal secures Thailand's export base and long-term economic stability. This is viewed as a strategic victory, maintaining the country's competitiveness in the U.S. market compared to its regional neighbors.

คู่มือการสอนและกิจกรรม / Teacher & Student Guide

อุ่นเครื่องก่อนอ่าน / Pre-Reading Warm-Up

ใช้คำถามเหล่านี้เพื่อกระตุ้นความสนใจและเปิดประเด็นกับนักเรียน
Use these questions to spark interest and start a conversation with students:

  • คุณรู้จักคำว่า "ภาษี" หรือ "Tariff" หรือไม่? ลองอธิบายตามความเข้าใจของคุณ
    Do you know the word "Tariff"? Try to explain it in your own words.
    Answer Guide: A tariff is a tax that a government puts on goods coming into the country from another country. This makes the imported goods more expensive. Governments use tariffs to protect their own companies from foreign competition.
  • คุณคิดว่าการค้าระหว่างประเทศไทยกับสหรัฐอเมริกามีความสำคัญอย่างไร?
    How important do you think trade between Thailand and the USA is?
    Answer Guide: The U.S. is one of Thailand's most important export markets. Many Thai goods, like electronics, food products (like seafood and rice), and automotive parts, are sold in the U.S. Strong trade helps Thailand's economy grow and creates jobs.

คำถามทดสอบความเข้าใจ / Comprehension Questions

หลังจากนักเรียนได้อ่านเนื้อหา ลองใช้คำถามเหล่านี้เพื่อประเมินความเข้าใจ
After students have read the text, use these questions to check their understanding:

สำหรับระดับพื้นฐาน (For Basic Level)

  • ประเทศไทยได้อัตราภาษีใหม่เท่าไหร่ และดีกว่าเดิมอย่างไร?
    What is Thailand's new tariff rate, and how is it better than before?
    Answer: Thailand's new tariff rate is 19%. It is better because it was reduced from a threatened rate of 36%.

สำหรับระดับสูง (For Advanced Level)

  • เหตุใดสหรัฐฯ จึงปฏิบัติต่อแคนาดาและเม็กซิโกแตกต่างกันในเรื่องภาษี?
    Why did the U.S. treat Canada and Mexico differently regarding tariffs?
    Answer: The U.S. increased tariffs on Canada due to a specific dispute over controlling fentanyl. In contrast, it granted Mexico a temporary reprieve (a delay) to allow for more comprehensive trade negotiations, showing a different diplomatic approach.
  • อธิบายว่าทำไมข้อตกลงนี้จึงถูกมองว่าเป็น "ชัยชนะเชิงกลยุทธ์" (strategic victory) สำหรับประเทศไทย?
    Explain why the deal is considered a "strategic victory" for Thailand.
    Answer: It's a "strategic victory" because it secures Thailand's economic stability and maintains its competitiveness in the U.S. market compared to its regional neighbors. By getting a 19% rate, which is similar to or better than some neighbors, Thailand avoids being at a disadvantage and protects its vital export industry.

แบบฝึกหัดเติมคำในช่องว่าง / Fill-in-the-Blanks Exercise

ให้นักเรียนเลือกคำจาก "คลังคำศัพท์" ไปเติมในประโยคให้ถูกต้อง
Have students choose a word from the "Word Bank" below to complete each sentence correctly.

announced | imposed | government | stability | significant | reduced
  1. The President ________ new tariffs on many countries.
  2. The Thai ________ was very happy with the new deal.
  3. The new rules were ________ to control imports.
  4. The company saw a ________ increase in profits this year.
  5. The new agreement helps ensure economic ________ for the country.
  6. Good negotiation ________ the potential tax rate from 36% to 19%.
Answer Key:
1. announced
2. government
3. imposed
4. significant
5. stability
6. reduced

หัวข้อสำหรับอภิปราย / Discussion Topics

ใช้คำถามเหล่านี้เพื่อต่อยอดการเรียนรู้และฝึกทักษะการแสดงความคิดเห็น
Use these questions to extend learning and practice expressing opinions.

  • ในความเห็นของคุณ อัตราภาษี 19% เป็นข้อตกลงที่ดีที่สุดสำหรับประเทศไทยแล้วหรือยัง? เพราะอะไร?
    In your opinion, is the 19% rate the best possible deal for Thailand? Why or why not?
    Guiding Points for Discussion: There is no single right answer. Students can argue "Yes" because it's a huge reduction from 36% and keeps Thailand competitive. They could also argue "No" by pointing out that other strategic partners of the U.S. might have lower rates (e.g., 10% or 15% in other deals), suggesting there might have been room for a better outcome.
  • นอกจากภาษีแล้ว มีปัจจัยอะไรอีกบ้างที่ส่งผลต่อการค้าระหว่างประเทศ?
    Besides tariffs, what other factors affect international trade?
    Guiding Points for Discussion: Students can brainstorm factors such as: exchange rates (the value of the baht vs. the dollar), shipping costs, product quality, trade agreements (like FTAs), political relationships between countries, and global events (like a pandemic or a war).
