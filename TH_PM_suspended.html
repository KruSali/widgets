ประเด็นร้อน: ศาลรัฐธรรมนูญสั่งนายกฯ หยุดปฏิบัติหน้าที่

เรื่องย่อ (ฉบับเข้าใจง่าย)

The Constitutional Court has ordered Prime Minister Paetongtarn Shinawatra to suspend her work. This is a temporary stop while the court looks at a case against her. The case is about a phone call that was leaked to the public. In the call, she talked to Cambodia's Senate President, Hun Sen. Some senators say she said bad things about a Thai army commander who is in charge of the border with Cambodia. They also said she sounded too willing to agree with Hun Sen. For now, Deputy Prime Minister Suriya Jungrungreangkit will be the acting prime minister. Ms. Paetongtarn said she made the call to stop problems at the border.

Summary (Advanced Version)

The Constitutional Court has suspended Prime Minister Paetongtarn Shinawatra from duty pending its consideration of a case against her. The court unanimously agreed to review a petition from senators concerning a leaked phone call with Cambodian Senate President Hun Sen. In the recording, Ms. Paetongtarn allegedly made derogatory remarks about a Thai army commander and displayed a submissive tone towards Hun Sen. The complaint accuses her of a grave breach of ethical standards and of lacking the required integrity for her office. Her tenure as prime minister has been suspended, and an acting PM has been appointed. Ms. Paetongtarn has 15 days to provide a written clarification, stating her intention was to prevent border clashes.
