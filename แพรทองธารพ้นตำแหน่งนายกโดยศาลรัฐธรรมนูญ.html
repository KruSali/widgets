The Constitutional Court has removed Prime Minister Paetongtarn Shinawatra from her job. The court voted 6 to 3. This happened because of a phone call she had with a leader from Cambodia.

The court said she broke a serious ethical rule. In the call, she talked about border problems between Thailand and Cambodia. The court believed her words were not careful. They said she seemed to put her own political problems before the country's safety.

Because of this decision, the entire cabinet must also leave their jobs.

สรุปข่าวเวอร์ชั่นขั้นสูง

In a landmark 6-3 ruling, the Constitutional Court has ousted Prime Minister Paetongtarn Shinawatra from office. The verdict cited a serious violation of the ethical code for political officeholders.

The case stemmed from a leaked audio recording of a conversation with Cambodian Senate President Hun Sen. The court ruled that her conduct constituted a grave ethical breach. Judges found her negotiation tactics, such as asking for sympathy and seemingly offering unconditional concessions, undermined national security and prioritized her government's stability over national interests.

As a direct consequence of her removal, the entire cabinet is also dissolved.
