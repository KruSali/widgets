สรุปสถานการณ์ไทย-กัมพูชา

ครูสาลี่ -- อังกฤษฉบับคนเรียนไม่รู้เรื่อง

อัปเดตล่าสุด: 27 กรกฎาคม 2568 (รายงานสดจากชายแดน)

สำหรับผู้เริ่มต้น (Beginners)

The fight between Thailand and Cambodia is now on its 4th day.
Donald Trump asked both sides to stop fighting and then talk about trade.
But Thai soldiers will only stop if Cambodia asks to talk first.
The fighting started when Cambodia shot at a Thai army base in Surin on July 24. Later, the fighting spread to Chanthaburi and Trat.
Thailand and Cambodia will talk in Malaysia tomorrow.
A Cambodian rocket hit Sisaket, killing 1 person and hurting 1. A casino near the border was shot at, causing smoke.
Thai soldiers returned 12 dead Cambodian soldiers to Cambodia. The fighting is still very tense. Thai soldiers took back a key area.
The Thai Air Force asked people not to share details about their weapons. People are leaving Cambodia and going back to Thailand.
Some hospitals and schools near the border are closed or moving patients.
The UN wants both sides to stop fighting and talk. Cambodian Prime Minister Hun Manet agreed to Trump's ceasefire idea. The Philippines offered to help Thailand and Cambodia.

สำหรับผู้เริ่มต้นระดับกลาง (Advanced Beginners)

The conflict between Thailand and Cambodia has escalated into its fourth day.
Donald Trump urged both nations to implement an immediate ceasefire and then resume trade negotiations.
However, the Thai army stated that troops would only cease fire if Cambodia initiated negotiations.
The hostilities began on July 24, when Cambodian forces fired upon a Thai military base in Surin. The fighting subsequently extended to Chanthaburi and Trat provinces.
Thailand and Cambodia are scheduled to hold talks in Malaysia tomorrow.
A Cambodian BM21 rocket landed in Sisaket, resulting in 1 fatality and 1 injury. A border casino was shelled, causing smoke to rise.
Thai forces repatriated the bodies of 12 deceased Cambodian soldiers to Cambodian authorities. The border situation remains highly tense, with Thai soldiers recapturing a strategic area.
The Royal Thai Air Force urged the public to refrain from sharing sensitive military information. Large numbers of Cambodian citizens are returning to their country through border checkpoints.
Border hospitals and schools are either closed indefinitely or evacuating patients.
The United Nations called for a ceasefire and peaceful negotiations between the two nations. Cambodian Prime Minister Hun Manet accepted Donald Trump's ceasefire proposal. The Philippines has offered assistance in de-escalating tensions and restoring peace.
