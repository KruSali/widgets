Thai-Cambodia Border Clash: Ongoing Conflict Affects Thai Civilians

ข่าว: การปะทะชายแดนไทย-กัมพูชา: ความขัดแย้งยังคงดำเนิน กระทบพลเรือนไทย

ครูสาลี่ -- อังกฤษฉบับคนเรียนไม่รู้เรื่อง

There was fighting between Thailand and Cambodia near Ta Muen Thom Temple. This fighting has hurt people in Thailand. The 2nd Army Area Operation Center said the fighting did not stop today, July 24, 2025. Cambodian soldiers started shooting near Moo Pa Base. Thai soldiers shot back.

In an area near Don Tual Temple, two Cambodian tanks were damaged. Cambodian soldiers also used rockets to shoot at Kantaralak District in Sisaket Province, hitting a gas station. Near Preah Vihear Temple, there was fighting from Wat Kaeo to Phu Makheua. Both sides are still in their positions.

At Chong Chom, Cambodian soldiers fired weapons to help attack Thai civilians areas. These areas include Sangkha Vocational College in Surin, the Thai-Cambodian Border Development Center, and Thai homes. At Ta Muen Thom Temple, after tourism was closed, Cambodian soldiers attacked Thai soldiers and kept shooting at civilian areas. The fighting has not stopped.

Because of the fighting, 11 areas in Thailand were affected. For example, Nong Raet Village in Surin had 10 artillery shells fall. Sadly, 32 people were impacted. 20 people were injured, and 12 people died.

The 2nd Army Area Operation Center asks Cambodia to cease attacking Thai civilian areas. They say this goes against international law that protects people. Thailand will only attack military targets. Thailand believes in the rule of law and human rights, but will not let anyone violate its sovereignty or harm its national pride.

Today, July 25, is the second day of fighting. Cambodian soldiers opened fire again this morning. The 2nd Army Area told people to avoid the border areas because of many clashes.

Reporters said that there was continuous clashes from yesterday evening (July 24) until late at night. Then at 3:25 AM today (July 25), an incident occurred near Preah Vihear Temple in Sisaket Province, with fighting.

In Kantaralak District, Sisaket Province, people were told to evacuate. They moved to shelter centers for safety. Now, villagers need essential personal items like toothpaste, soap, and dry food. Drinking water supplies are ample.

At 9:00 AM today, the Army got a report that hostilities commenced at 4:00 AM in Chong Bok, Ubon Ratchathani, and Phu Makheua, Sisaket. It also happened in Phanom Dong Rak District, Surin. Cambodian soldiers launched heavy weapons, field artillery, and BM-21 rockets. Thai soldiers retaliated with supporting fire as the situation dictated.

The Army asks people in these areas to refrain from entering the fighting zones.

General Pana Klaewplodtook, the Army Commander, is leading an operation called "Yuthabodin." It is still ongoing, utilizing ground forces and air support in the operational zones.

A border conflict has occurred between Thailand and Cambodia near Ta Muen Thom Temple, causing damage and injuries on the Thai side. The 2nd Army Area Operation Center reported on July 24, 2025, that the situation remains tense. Cambodian troops initiated gunfire near Moo Pa Base, prompting Thai soldiers to return fire.

Near Don Tual Temple, two Cambodian tanks were rendered inoperable. Cambodian forces also launched BM-21 rockets into Kantaralak District, Sisaket Province, hitting a gas station. Heavy fighting also took place from Wat Kaeo to Phu Makheua near Preah Vihear Temple. Both sides are currently holding their ground.

At Chong Chom, Cambodian soldiers used supporting fire against Thai civilian locations, including Sangkha Vocational College in Surin, the Thai-Cambodian Border Development Center, and homes. After tourism was closed at Ta Muen Thom Temple, Cambodian forces launched an offensive against Thai positions and continued to shell civilian areas. There is no sign of the conflict ending soon.

The conflict has affected 11 areas in Thailand. For instance, Nong Raet Village in Surin experienced 10 artillery shells impacts. In total, 32 Thai civilians have been impacted, with 20 sustaining non-fatal injuries and 12 fatalities reported.

The 2nd Army Area Operation Center has urged Cambodia to cease attacks on Thai civilian areas, stating that such actions violate humanitarian law. Thailand maintains its commitment to the rule of law and human rights, but will not allow its sovereignty to be violated or its national dignity undermined.

Entering its second day, Cambodian forces resumed firing this morning, July 25. The 2nd Army Area has issued a warning to the public to avoid border areas following multiple clashes.

Reporters noted that from yesterday evening (July 24) until late into the night, consistent clashes were heard along the border. By 3:25 AM today (July 25), intense hostilities were reported near Preah Vihear Temple in Sisaket Province.

Kantaralak District in Sisaket Province has been declared an evacuation zone, with residents directed to relocate to shelter centers for their safety. Currently, villagers are in need of essential personal items such as toothpaste, soap, and dried food, though drinking water supplies are ample.

As of 9:00 AM today, the Royal Thai Army received reports from ground units indicating that hostilities commenced at 4:00 AM in Chong Bok, Ubon Ratchathani, Phu Makheua, Sisaket, and Phanom Dong Rak District, Surin. Cambodian forces intensified their barrage with heavy weaponry, field artillery pieces, and continuous BM-21 rocket fire. The Thai side responded with support fire as the situation dictated.

The Royal Thai Army reiterates its advisory to civilians to refrain from entering active combat areas.

Furthermore, reports indicate that Operation Yuthabodin, under the command of Army Commander Gen. Pana Klaewplodtook as the Incident Commander, remains ongoing, utilizing ground forces and air support in the operational zones.
